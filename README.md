### Questions Emerged from Research

After mapping all 12 hallmarks of aging ([full analysis](research/02_all_12_hallmarks_of_aging.md)), new strategic questions surfaced:

#### 6. Strategy: Leaders vs. Gaps
Do we focus on the **most advanced** hallmarks (nutrient sensing, inflammation — approved drugs already exist) or on the **most under-researched** ones (genomic instability, intercellular communication — higher potential for breakthroughs)?

#### 7. Integration as a Niche
Nobody is systematically researching how hallmarks interconnect. Building a **dependency graph** and finding interventions that hit multiple hallmarks at once could be a uniquely valuable contribution. Is this our angle?

#### 8. Regulatory Blocker
The FDA does not recognize aging as a disease — this blocks direct clinical trials for longevity. Do we research workarounds (specific diseases as proxies, like the TAME trial does with metformin) or focus on advocacy for regulatory change?

#### 9. Planning Horizon
What timeframe are we working toward?
- **5 years** — what's realistically achievable now
- **20 years** — long-term strategy
- **Open-ended** — until the problem is solved
